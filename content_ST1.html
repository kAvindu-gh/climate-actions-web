Climate Action: Deforestation

Causes Effects Solutions

Causes of Deforestation

Deforestation is driven by multiple human activities. The main contributors include agriculture (especially cattle ranching and palm oil plantations), illegal logging, urban expansion, and mining. Forests are cleared for crop cultivation, infrastructure, and commercial development, especially in tropical regions like the Amazon and Southeast Asia.

Effects of Deforestation

The loss of forests leads to habitat destruction, species extinction, climate imbalance, and increased greenhouse gas emissions. Deforestation contributes to global warming by reducing carbon absorption, and increases the risk of soil erosion, floods, and desertification.

Solutions to Deforestation

Solutions include reforestation, enforcing anti-logging laws, promoting sustainable agriculture, and supporting eco-friendly products. Consumers can help by reducing meat consumption, avoiding products with unsustainable palm oil, and planting trees locally.
